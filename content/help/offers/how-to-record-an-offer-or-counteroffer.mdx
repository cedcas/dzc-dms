## What is an offer?

An offer is a formal settlement proposal — either sent by DZC to a creditor (outgoing) or received from a creditor (incoming). Offers track the settlement amount, payment type, and status throughout the negotiation.

## How to record an offer

1. Navigate to the **Debt Account** detail page
2. Scroll to the **Offers** section
3. Click **New Offer**
4. Fill in the offer details:
   - **Direction** — Outgoing (we sent it) or Incoming (we received it)
   - **Amount** — the settlement dollar amount
   - **Percentage of Balance** — e.g., `45.00` for 45% (optional, auto-calculated if you prefer)
   - **Payment Type** — Lump Sum or Installment
   - **Installment Count and Frequency** — if installment, specify how many payments and how often
   - **Source** — creditor rep name or DZC contact name
   - **Expires At** — deadline for acceptance if applicable
   - **Notes** — any context about the offer
5. Click **Save Offer**

## Offer statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **Pending** | Offer is open and awaiting response |
| **Accepted** | Offer has been accepted by both parties |
| **Rejected** | Offer was declined |
| **Countered** | The other party responded with a counteroffer |
| **Expired** | Offer deadline passed without acceptance |

## Updating an offer status

Once an offer is created, you can update its status from the Offers table on the account detail page:
1. Find the offer in the Offers table
2. Use the status dropdown or action button to update it
3. The **Responded At** timestamp is set automatically when you change the status

## Business rule: one accepted offer per account

Only one offer can have the status **Accepted** per debt account at a time. If you try to accept a second offer, the system will warn you and ask if you want to replace the previously accepted offer.

## Recording a counteroffer

When you receive a counter:
1. Update the original offer status to **Countered**
2. Create a new offer record for the counteroffer with the new amount
3. Set direction to **Incoming** if the creditor counter'd, or **Outgoing** if you are countering back

## Tips

- Always record the percentage of balance alongside the dollar amount — this is what gets reported
- Use Notes to capture any verbal agreements or creditor reference numbers
- Mark expired offers promptly so the Offers table does not get cluttered with stale records

## Common mistakes

- Recording an accepted offer without updating the account status to Settled — do both together
- Forgetting to log the corresponding negotiation activity (Offer Sent / Offer Received) — always log both
